Facundo Buonanotte: Chelsea Loan Explained, Permanent Prospects, and the Messi Talk

Published September 3, 2025

Argentine talent Facundo Buonanotte has joined Chelsea on a season-long loan from Brighton & Hove Albion. Below, we break down what this move means, whether it can become permanent, and why he often draws comparisons to Lionel Messi.

Chelsea Loan: Can It Become Permanent?

Buonanotte’s move is a season-long loan. Current reporting and club messaging indicate no option or obligation to buy is included in the agreement, and Brighton have publicly framed his long-term future as remaining with them. Practically, that means any permanent transfer would require a fresh negotiation after the loan, if all parties are interested.

  • Status now: Loan only, through the 2025/26 season.
  • Path to permanent: Not built into this deal; would need new talks later.

Is He Compared to Messi?

Comparisons to Lionel Messi have surfaced since Buonanotte’s breakout at Rosario Central. Notably, Carlos Tévez, who coached him there, once said that when Buonanotte “brakes and accelerates,” he reminds him of Messi. These remarks speak to Facundo’s low-center-of-gravity dribbling, sharp acceleration, and left-footed creativity.

That said, Buonanotte himself has played down direct comparisons, acknowledging Messi’s unique status. Think of the comp as a style reference, not a 1:1 equivalence.
