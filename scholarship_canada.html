UBC Opens 2026 Mastercard Foundation Scholars Graduate Admissions

By Aidoko News Desk · Published September 3, 2025

The University of British Columbia (UBC) has announced application timelines and eligibility for the Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program (Graduate, Winter 2026 intake), including an Expression of Interest window, program list, and step-by-step application stages.

Key Dates (2025–2026)

  • Aug 30, 2025: Expression of Interest opens
  • By Sep 12, 2025: Submit Expression of Interest (General & Refugees streams)
  • Sep 15 – Oct 15, 2025: Round 1 invitations sent to eligible applicants
  • Nov 2025: Round 2 – apply to UBC faculty/program
  • Dec 2025 – Jan 2026: Interviews (Round 3)
  • Feb 2026: Offers issued for Winter term
  • Aug 2026: Scholars arrive in Vancouver

Source: UBC program pages.

What the Scholarship Covers

  • Tuition & books
  • Flights & immigration support
  • Room & board; stipend for living expenses
  • Health insurance; career services & support
  • Access to a global Mastercard Foundation alumni network
  • Social Entrepreneurship Fund

Who Can Apply (Eligibility Snapshot)

  • Bachelor’s degree completed
  • No previous Master’s degree
  • Age ≤ 35 at time of application
  • Citizen or refugee of an African country
  • Meets UBC minimum academic requirements and (if required) English proficiency
  • Applying to one of the eligible UBC programs below

Eligible UBC Programs

Faculty of Land & Food Systems

  • Master of Food & Resource Economics (MFRE)
  • Master of Food Science (MFS)
  • Master of Land & Water Systems (MLWS)

Faculty of Forestry

  • Master of Sustainable Forest Management (MSFM)
  • Master of International Forestry (MIF)
  • Master of Geomatics for Environmental Management (MGEM)
  • MSc/MASc in Forestry (thesis-based)

Faculty of Science

  • Master of Data Science (MDS)

How to Apply

  1. Decide your program(s) from the eligible list.
  2. Submit Expression of Interest by Sep 12, 2025: General · Refugees Only
  3. Round 1 (invitation-only): complete the Scholars Program application (form, CV, two reference letters on letterhead, transcripts).
  4. Round 2: if selected, apply to your UBC faculty/program (fee waiver guidance available; see UBC instructions).
  5. Round 3: interviews; offers released in February 2026.
